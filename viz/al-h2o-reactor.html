HOW IT WORKS
Stage 1: Aluminum Pellet Feed

Recycled aluminum (soda cans, scrap) is shredded into pebble-sized pellets and pre-treated with a thin coating of gallium-indium alloy. This alloy strips the natural oxide layer that normally prevents aluminum from reacting with water.

The pellets are stored in a hopper magazine — a sealed, gravity-fed cartridge that can be swapped at fuel stations like a propane tank. No volatile gases. No cryogenic storage. Just metal pellets at room temperature.

A motorized auger feeds pellets into the reaction chamber at a rate controlled by the vehicle's power demand. More throttle = more pellets = more hydrogen.
